SHOW / SAY / DO Framework

SHOW ‚Äî What We Put on Screen

  • Real try-ons (kids wiggling toes, teens checking mirrors, parents testing heel slip)
  • Lived-in family moments (muddy shoes by the door, car chaos, school drop-off rush)
  • Local context (slush puddles, Qu√©bec spring weather, neighborhood parks)
  • Imperfect-but-good energy (authentic, not art-directed)
The Rule: If it looks like an ad, it's not Rubino.

SAY ‚Äî How We Write and Talk

  • Mission-first language ("If you're doing spring reset..." not "New arrivals!")
  • Plain cues (no jargon, no industry speak)
  • Qu√©bec-first tone (weather truth, school rhythms, local humour)
  • Confidence-building guidance ("Do this, not that")
The Rule: Clear over clever. Guidance over hype.

DO ‚Äî What We Actually Make

  • Mission frames (back-to-school, first snow, size-up, prom prep)
  • Fit confidence (heel slip test, toe room check, traction demos)
  • Value signals (cost-per-wear, durability cues, versatility)
  • Creator everyday stories (real families, real routines, real proof)
  • After-care support (cleaning tips, break-in guidance, when to replace)
The Rule: Every post needs a mission. Every mission needs proof.

The 10 Rubinomandments

Guiding principles for content that builds consideration + store intent. We aim to be distinctively Rubino: fresh and approachable, helpful without the fluff, family-friendly without the cheese.

01Social is for People. We're Guests.

People open social to escape, connect, and be entertained‚Äînot to shop. If we show up like a flyer, they scroll. If we show up like a helpful human, we earn attention.

‚úÖ Talk like a person. Be useful, relatable, or funny.‚ùå Post like a catalogue.
02Be a Shoe Authority, Not a Salesperson.

Rubino wins when it reduces uncertainty: fit, comfort, sizing, value, weather reality. Confidence converts‚Äîand it converts best in-store.

‚úÖ "Do this, not that." Basics. Fit tests. Care tips.‚ùå "Here's a product, buy it."
03Reduce Mental Load or Don't Post.

Your audience is busy (family life, work, routines). Content that simplifies decisions gets saved, shared, and trusted.

‚úÖ Checklists, formulas, quick decision guides.‚ùå Vague inspiration that adds choices.
04The "Save" Button is Our North Star.

Saves and shares are the clearest signal of consideration: "I'll come back to this when I need it." That's intent-building.

‚úÖ Screenshot-able tips, carousels, short Reels with on-screen text.‚ùå Long captions that bury the point.
Example
05Real Humans Beat Perfect Production.

Polished often reads as "ad." Real reads as "truth." Rubino is a store brand with real expertise‚Äîhumans make that believable.

‚úÖ Staff, parents, kids, real-life try-ons, real constraints.‚ùå Over-styled, overly branded, overly staged.
06Guide Choices. Curate the Options.

Rubino's edge isn't "infinite selection." It's helping you pick the right thing. Curation is the value.

‚úÖ "If you're doing X, choose Y." "3 best boots for‚Ä¶"‚ùå Dump 12 options with no recommendation.
07Every Post Needs a Mission.

Mission-led content is instantly relevant: back-to-school, first snow, first walkers, size-up, sports season. Relevance beats novelty.

‚úÖ Build posts around a specific moment + specific problem.‚ùå Post random product because it's new.
08Series Are How We Win Consistently.

One-off posts are fragile. Series create habits: audiences know what they're getting, and teams know what to make.

‚úÖ 2‚Äì3 repeatable franchises tied to missions.‚ùå Reinvent the wheel every week.
09EGC is a Superpower‚ÄîIf It's Character-Driven.

For store-based brands, employee content is the fastest way to feel local, real, and trusted.

‚úÖ Staff as recurring characters + micro-expertise.‚ùå Employees reading brand lines like robots.
10Qu√©bec Context is Not a Filter‚ÄîIt's the Strategy.

Cultural relevance isn't decoration. It's how Rubino feels "for us"‚Äîseasons, weather swings, family realities, local references.

‚úÖ Qu√©bec seasons, school rhythms, winter truth, family-store nostalgia.‚ùå Generic North American retail content.

ElementDetails

Why EGC Matters

For a store-based brand, employee content is the fastest way to feel local, real, and trusted. It humanizes the brand and demonstrates the expertise customers experience in-store.

EGC Wins When It's...

  • Character-driven: Recurring faces, personalities
  • Relatable: Weather frustrations, parent moments, shoe truths
  • Authentic: Not polished, not scripted brand lines

How to Encourage Participation

Make it Easy

  • Provide simple prompts (not scripts)
  • Share examples of what works
  • Create a submission process (shared folder, WhatsApp group)

Make it Rewarding

  • Feature top contributors on main account
  • Internal recognition (shoutouts, leaderboard)
  • No pressure ‚Äî voluntary means voluntary
